SIMPLE LOOKUP QUESTIONS (single section, direct answer)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Q: What is the GST rate applied to the premium?
A: 18%
   Source: Section 5.13 — GST is levied at 18% on the final premium computed
   after Step 7 of Clause 5.7.

Q: What is the Free-Look Period for this policy?
A: 30 days from receipt of the Policy document (for policies with tenure of
   one year or more), during which the Policyholder may cancel for a full refund
   less proportionate risk premium for the period on cover and any medical test
   costs incurred.
   Source: Clause 7.3

Q: What is the Grace Period for premium payment?
A: 30 days (for annual policies). Continuity of coverage (including waiting
   periods already served) is preserved, though cover itself is suspended during
   the grace period unless premium is paid.
   Source: Clause 7.4

Q: How many Day Care Procedures are covered?
A: 148 procedures (including cataract surgery, dialysis, chemotherapy, and
   lithotripsy), payable on par with inpatient hospitalization per Clause 3.4.
   Source: Clause 4.5

Q: What is the Moratorium Period?
A: 8 years of continuous coverage from the first policy inception date, after
   which the insurer cannot contest the policy except for fraud.
   Source: Clause 2.6

Q: What is the Initial Waiting Period?
A: 30 days from first inception for all claims except those arising from an
   accident, which are covered from Day 1.
   Source: Clause 6.2

Q: What is the Pre-Existing Disease (PED) Waiting Period?
A: 48 months of continuous coverage from the first policy inception date for
   the specific Insured Person, after which such disease is covered.
   Source: Clause 6.1

TABLE-BASED QUESTIONS (require reading rate/benefit tables)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Q: What is the Room Rent Sub-Limit per day for a Band A policy in Zone B?
A: INR 3,200 per day
   Source: Section 4.6 — Room Rent & ICU Sub-Limit Table

Q: What is the base premium rate for an Individual plan in Band C (Column C)?
A: 6.90 (Rate per Mille of Sum Insured, per Annum)
   Source: Section 5.2 — Base Rate Table

Q: What is the Cumulative Bonus percentage after 3 consecutive claim-free years?
A: 30% increase in Sum Insured (cumulative, of original Band Sum Insured)
   Source: Section 4.10 — Cumulative Bonus Table

Q: What is the Zone Multiplier for Zone A (metro)?
A: 1.20
   Source: Section 5.3 — Zone Multiplier Table

Q: What age loading applies to a 55-year-old (AG4 band)?
A: 55% loading on the Zone-adjusted Base Rate
   Source: Section 5.4 — Age Band Loading Table

Q: What is the Long-Term Policy Discount for a 3-year policy term?
A: 10% discount on the cumulative annual premium (paid upfront)
   Source: Section 5.12 — Long-Term Policy Discount Table

Q: What is the Restoration Benefit?
A: Automatic reinstatement of the base Sum Insured, once per Policy Period,
   after it has been exhausted by claims, subject to conditions in Clause 4.11.
   Source: Clause 2.5

MULTI-SECTION / CROSS-REFERENCE QUESTIONS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Q: If a 60-year-old in Zone A with Band B has a PED loading of 30% and also
   qualifies for Zone-based co-payment, are these additive?
A: Yes, Zone-based and Senior Citizen co-payments are ADDITIVE (per
   Footnote 4.9-a). This is unlike the Long-Term Discount logic in 5.12 which
   is NOT combinable with Band-change rating.
   Source: Sections 4.8, 4.9, Footnote 4.9-a

Q: If someone has two pre-existing conditions with individual loadings of 30%
   and 20%, what is the combined PED loading?
A: 55% (not 50%). Footnote 5.9-b gives an explicit non-additive combined
   PED loading that contradicts a naive arithmetic sum.
   Source: Section 5.9, Footnote 5.9-b

Q: What documentation is required for a reimbursement claim?
A: Duly filled claim form, original discharge summary, original hospital bills
   with itemized break-up, original payment receipts, investigation/diagnostic
   reports, original pharmacy bills with prescriptions, KYC documents, and for
   Critical Illness claims a specialist's certificate confirming diagnosis and
   Survival Period.
   Source: Clause 8.4

Q: How does the Family Floater Sum Insured work across members?
A: The Sum Insured Band operates as a single pool across all named family
   members; each member's claim reduces the shared balance, and the Cumulative
   Bonus accrues to the shared pool, not per individual.
   Source: Clause 1.2(b)

Q: What happens to the Cumulative Bonus if a claim is made?
A: A single claim in any Policy Period reduces the accrued Cumulative Bonus
   by 10 percentage points at the next renewal (not to below 0%); it does NOT
   reset entirely to 0% on a single claim.
   Source: Footnote 4.10-a

COMPARISON / LOOKUP QUESTIONS (test table reading accuracy)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Q: Compare the Room Rent Sub-Limits for Band A vs Band C in Zone A.
A: Band A Zone A = INR 4,000/day; Band C Zone A = INR 10,000/day.
   Source: Section 4.6

Q: Which Band has the highest Family Floater (2 adults + kids) base rate?
A: Band A (Column A) = 17.80 per Mille
   Source: Section 5.2 — Base Rate Table

Q: What is the difference in waiting periods for Cataract vs Initial claims?
A: Cataract has a 2-year disease-specific waiting period from first inception
   (Section 6.3). Initial waiting period is 30 days for all non-accident
   claims (Section 6.2). Cataract must serve BOTH periods.
   Source: Sections 6.2, 6.3

EDGE CASE / TRICKY QUESTIONS (test retrieval depth)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Q: Can the Long-Term Discount be combined with a mid-term Band upgrade?
A: No. Footnote 5.12-a states the discount is NOT combinable with any
   mid-term Band upgrade under Clause 7.6, which requires a fresh 1-year rate
   calculation for the incremental Sum Insured only.
   Source: Footnote 5.12-a

Q: Does the Cumulative Bonus increase the premium-paying amount?
A: No. Cumulative Bonus increases the Sum Insured available for claims but
   does NOT increase the premium-paying amount.
   Source: Footnote 4.10-b

Q: What is the claim settlement timeline?
A: Per IRDAI (Protection of Policyholders' Interests) Regulations, cashless
   pre-authorization decision must be communicated within 1 hour of receipt of
   all required information. Final claim settlement within 15 working days of
   receipt of all required documents.
   Source: Clause 8.5
