SCENARIO A: MULTI-HOP CLAIMS ADJUDICATION
(Uses MediCare Plus policy — Health Insurance)
These questions require retrieving facts from multiple sections and reasoning
across them to reach a conclusion.
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Q: A 55-year-old in Zone A with Band C is hospitalized. The hospital charges
   INR 7,000/day for a room. How does the Room Rent Sub-Limit and
   Proportionate Deduction apply?
   A: Band C has No Room Rent Sub-Limit (up to Single Private Room) per
      Section 4.6, so if the room category is Single Private Room or below,
      no proportionate deduction applies. However, if the room exceeds that
      category, Clause 4.7's Proportionate Deduction formula kicks in —
      reducing Room Rent, Surgeon Fees, Anaesthetist Fees, OT Charges, and
      Specialist Fees proportionally, but NOT Medicines, Consumables, or
      Diagnostic Tests (which are paid in full).
      Requires cross-referencing: Section 4.6 (sub-limits) + Section 4.7
      (proportionate deduction) + Footnote 4.7-a (category exception).

Q: A 63-year-old Senior Citizen in Zone B with Band D makes a claim. What
   co-payment percentage applies and when is it calculated in the claim
   settlement process?
   A: Age 61-65 falls in the 10% co-payment tier per Section 4.8. The
      co-payment is applied AFTER the Proportionate Deduction under Clause
      4.7 (if applicable) and after any Sub-Limit deduction — it is
      calculated on the net admissible amount, not the gross claimed amount
      (Footnote 4.8-a). Zone B does not trigger Zone-Based Co-payment
      (Section 4.9 only applies when treated in a different Zone).
      Requires cross-referencing: Section 4.8 + Footnote 4.8-a +
      Section 4.9 + Section 4.7.

Q: A policyholder rated in Zone C gets treatment at a Zone A hospital. They
   are also 68 years old (Senior Citizen variant). What co-payments apply
   and are they additive?
   A: Two co-payments apply: (1) Zone-Based Co-payment of 10% per Section
      4.9 (Zone C-rated treated in Zone A), AND (2) Senior Citizen
      Co-payment of 15% per Section 4.8 (age 66-70). Per Footnote 4.9-a,
      these two co-payments are ADDITIVE — they stack, unlike the Long-Term
      Discount logic in Clause 5.12 which is NOT combinable with Band-change
      rating.
      Requires cross-referencing: Section 4.9 + Section 4.8 + Footnote
      4.9-a + Section 5.12.

Q: A policyholder has had 3 consecutive claim-free years (accruing 30%
   Cumulative Bonus), then makes a claim in the 4th year. What happens to
   the bonus at next renewal, and does the Restoration Benefit also apply?
   A: Per Footnote 4.10-a, a single claim reduces the accrued Cumulative
      Bonus by 10 percentage points at the next renewal (not to below 0%),
      so 30% becomes 20%. The bonus does NOT reset to 0%. Separately, if
      the Sum Insured (including bonus) is exhausted, the Restoration
      Benefit under Clause 4.11 restores 100% of the original Band Sum
      Insured once — but only for unrelated claims (Footnote 4.11-b).
      Restoration is NOT available on Band A (Footnote 4.11-a).
      Requires cross-referencing: Section 4.10 + Footnote 4.10-a +
      Section 4.11 + Footnotes 4.11-a, 4.11-b.

Q: What documentation is required for a reimbursement claim involving a
   Critical Illness diagnosis?
   A: Per Section 8.4, the checklist includes: duly filled claim form,
      original discharge summary, original hospital bills with itemized
      break-up, original payment receipts, investigation/diagnostic reports,
      original pharmacy bills with prescriptions, KYC documents, and for
      Critical Illness claims (Clause 4.3), a specialist's certificate
      confirming diagnosis and Survival Period.
      Requires cross-referencing: Section 8.4 + Section 4.3.

Q: If someone has both controlled hypertension and controlled Type 2
   diabetes, what is the combined PED loading? How does this differ from
   simply adding the individual loadings?
   A: Per Section 5.9, controlled hypertension = +20% and controlled
      Type 2 diabetes = +30%. However, Footnote 5.9-b explicitly states
      the combined loading for "both hypertension and diabetes" is +55% —
      NOT 20%+30%=50% recalculated, nor +50%. This is a non-additive cap
      that contradicts naive arithmetic. Any PED combination not expressly
      listed is referred to Underwriting per Note 9.4.
      Requires cross-referencing: Section 5.9 + Footnote 5.9-b + Note 9.4.

Q: Can the Critical Illness Rider be attached for a 62-year-old in the
   Senior Citizen variant? What are the implications?
   A: No. Per Section 5.10, the CI Rider is "Not available — Rider cannot
      be attached to Senior Citizen variant per Clause 1.2(c)" for Age
      Bands AG6 (61-65) and AG7 (66+). Additionally, per Note 9.6, the
      Rider may only be attached at proposal stage or at renewal, not
      mid-Policy-Period.
      Requires cross-referencing: Section 5.10 + Clause 1.2(c) + Note 9.6.

Q: A Band B policyholder's hospital room rent exceeds the sub-limit. Which
   expenses are proportionately deducted and which are paid in full?
   A: Per Section 4.7, the Proportionate Deduction applies to: Room Rent
      itself, Surgeon Fees, Anaesthetist Fees, OT Charges, and Specialist
      Fees. It does NOT apply to Medicines, Consumables, or Diagnostic Test
      charges, which are payable in full regardless of the room rent breach.
      Additionally, Footnote 4.7-a states the deduction does not apply if
      admitted to Single Private Room category or below at that hospital.
      Requires cross-referencing: Section 4.7 + Section 4.6 + Footnote
      4.7-a + Note 9.7.

Q: A policyholder wants to upgrade from Band B to Band D mid-term and also
   has a 3-year Long-Term Discount. Can both be applied simultaneously?
   A: No. Footnote 5.12-a states the Long-Term Discount is NOT combinable
      with any mid-term Band upgrade under Clause 7.6, which requires a
      fresh 1-year rate calculation for the incremental Sum Insured only.
      Additionally, per Clause 7.6, Band changes are only permitted at
      renewal, not mid-term.
      Requires cross-referencing: Footnote 5.12-a + Clause 7.6.

Q: What is the Maternity Benefit waiting period and how does it interact
   with the Initial Waiting Period for a new policyholder on Band D?
   A: The Maternity Waiting Period is 36 months per Section 6.4/Clause 4.4.
      The Initial Waiting Period is 30 days per Section 6.2. These run
      independently — the 36-month Maternity wait is the binding constraint.
      Per Footnote 4.4-b, the 36-month wait runs continuously across
      renewals provided there is no break exceeding the Grace Period (30
      days per Clause 7.4); a lapse resets it to zero. Maternity is only
      available on Band C, D, or E (Clause 4.4).
      Requires cross-referencing: Section 6.4 + Clause 4.4 + Section 6.2 +
      Footnote 4.4-b + Clause 7.4.

Q: If an Insured Person permanently relocates from Zone A (Mumbai) to Zone C
   mid-Policy-Period, how does this affect their Room Rent Sub-Limit and
   premium rating?
   A: Per Clause 7.11 and Footnote 4.6-a, the Zone used for RATING
      (premium calculation) remains unchanged until the next renewal.
      However, the Room Rent Sub-Limit applicable to a CLAIM is determined
      by the Zone of the hospital where treatment is received. So the
      policyholder's premium stays at Zone A rates, but if treated in a
      Zone C hospital, the Room Rent Sub-Limit follows Zone C Band rates
      (e.g., Band A drops from INR 4,000 to INR 2,500/day). These two
      Zone references can diverge and are deliberately NOT reconciled.
      Requires cross-referencing: Clause 7.11 + Footnote 4.6-a + Section
      4.6 + Section 5.1.

SCENARIO A (CONTINUED): MULTI-HOP CLAIMS — MOTOR INSURANCE
(Uses DriveSecure Motor Insurance policy)
These questions require cross-referencing multiple sections of the motor
policy to reach a conclusion.
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Q: A 7-year-old car with EC3 engine is declared total loss. The owner had
   40% NCB and the IDV is INR 6,50,000. What is the OD settlement amount?
   A: Steps:
      1. IDV = INR 6,50,000 per Clause 4.1 (for a 7-year-old car, depreciation = 50%, but IDV already accounts for it).
      2. Total Loss = constructive total loss under Clause 8.12 (repair cost exceeds 75% of IDV).
      3. NCB discount = 40% applied on the own damage premium, NOT on the IDV.
      4. Own Damage premium = (IDV × own damage rate) − NCB discount.
      Requires cross-referencing: Clause 4.1 (IDV) + Clause 8.12 (total loss) + Clause 5.6 (NCB).

Q: A policyholder has a voluntary deductible of INR 15,000 and an NCB of
   50%. What is the effective discount on the own damage premium?
   A: Step 1: 50% NCB per Clause 5.6.
      Step 2: 20% discount for voluntary deductible of INR 15,000 per Clause 5.9.
      These are additive (both apply on the own damage premium).
      Effective discount = 50% + 20% = 70% on OD premium.
      Requires cross-referencing: Clause 5.6 + Clause 5.9.

Q: A 3-year-old car suffers partial damage. The repair cost is INR 2,80,000
   and the IDV is INR 8,00,000. Is this a total loss or partial loss? What
   is the claim settlement amount?
   A: Repair cost (INR 2,80,000) = 35% of IDV (INR 8,00,000). Since 35% < 75%
      threshold under Clause 8.12, this is a PARTIAL LOSS, not total loss.
      The insurer pays repair cost minus deductible (Clause 8.3) and any
      applicable depreciation on parts.
      Requires cross-referencing: Clause 8.12 + Clause 8.3 + Clause 4.3 (depreciation).

Q: What Third Party Liability premium applies for a vehicle with engine
   capacity 1498cc (EC3) in a private car category?
   A: Per Section 5.7, Third Party Liability premium for EC3 (1000-1500cc)
      Class 1 (Private Car) = INR 3,522. This is a compulsory statutory
      premium — no NCB or voluntary deductible discount applies.
      Source: Section 5.7 — Third Party Premium Table.

Q: What is the IDV depreciation for a vehicle that is 18 months old?
   A: Per Clause 4.1, vehicles 12-24 months old have 20% depreciation.
      IDV = Manufacturer's listed selling price − 20%.
      Source: Clause 4.1 — IDV Depreciation Table.

Q: A car owner with 30% NCB makes a claim for partial damage. The OD premium
   before NCB is INR 15,000 and the claim is for INR 25,000. What is the
   net payout?
   A: Step 1: NCB discount on OD premium = 30% of INR 15,000 = INR 4,500.
      Step 2: Net OD premium = INR 15,000 − INR 4,500 = INR 10,500.
      Step 3: The claim payout is limited to actual repair cost minus
      deductible (Clause 8.3). NCB affects premium, not payout.
      Requires cross-referencing: Clause 5.6 (NCB) + Clause 8.3 (deductible).

SCENARIO B: BENEFIT TABLES & RATE CHARTS
(Uses MediCare Plus policy — Health Insurance)
These questions require reading specific rate charts, benefit tables, and
extracting precise values.
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Q: What is the maximum Sum Insured available under this policy?
   A: Band E = INR 50,00,000 (50 Lakhs)
      Source: Section 4.1 — Sum Insured Bands Table

Q: What are the base premium rates for a Family Floater (2 adults + kids)
   across all columns?
   A: Column A (Band A): 17.80, Column B (Band B): 16.70,
      Column C (Band C): 15.10, Column D (Band D): 13.40,
      Column E (Band E): 11.90 (all rates per Mille of Sum Insured,
      per Annum)
      Source: Section 5.2 — Base Rate Table

Q: How does the Zone Multiplier affect premium calculation for Zone C
   customers?
   A: Zone C multiplier is 1.00 (no adjustment). Applied in Step 2 of the
      Calculation Sequence (Clause 5.7), BEFORE the Age Loading.
      Source: Section 5.3 — Zone Multiplier Table

Q: What age loading applies to a 50-year-old (AG4 band)?
   A: +55% loading on the Zone-adjusted Base Rate.
      Source: Section 5.4 — Age Band Loading Table

Q: What are the Pre-Policy Medical Test requirements for a 50-year-old
   applying for Band D?
   A: Per Section 5.11, entry age 46-55 with Band C/D/E requires: CBC,
      Urine Routine, ECG, and Fasting Blood Sugar tests.
      Source: Section 5.11 — Pre-Policy Medical Test Requirement Table

Q: What is the cumulative waiting period for a Cataract surgery claim?
   A: Cataract has a 2-year disease-specific waiting period from first
      inception (Section 6.3), PLUS the 30-day Initial Waiting Period
      (Section 6.2). Per Note 9.9, where a condition on the Section 6.3
      list is also a disclosed PED, the LONGER of the two waiting periods
      applies.
      Source: Sections 6.2, 6.3 + Note 9.9

Q: What is the Senior Citizen co-payment percentage for someone aged 65+?
   A: Age 61-65: 10%; Age 66-70: 15%; Age 71-75: 20%; Age 76+: 25%.
      Source: Section 4.8 — Senior Citizen Co-payment Table

Q: How does the Long-Term Policy Discount work for a 2-year term?
   A: 7.5% discount on the sum of annualized premiums for the full term,
      paid upfront. NOT combinable with mid-term Band upgrade under
      Clause 7.6.
      Source: Section 5.12 — Long-Term Policy Discount Table

Q: What is the Critical Illness Rider premium for a 40-year-old (AG3)?
   A: 1.85 per Mille of Rider Sum Insured, per Annum. Not subject to
      Zone Multiplier or PED Loading (Footnote 5.10-a). Not available for
      Senior Citizen variant (AG6-AG7).
      Source: Section 5.10 — Critical Illness Rider Rate Table

Q: If a 45-year-old in Zone B has Band B, what is the Zone-adjusted Base
   Rate before Age Loading?
   A: Base Rate (Band B, Column B, Individual) = 7.65 per Mille.
      Zone B Multiplier = 1.05.
      Zone-adjusted Base Rate = 7.65 x 1.05 = 8.0325 per Mille.
      Source: Section 5.2 (Base Rate) + Section 5.3 (Zone Multiplier)

Q: What are the disease-specific waiting periods for Benign Prostatic
   Hypertrophy and Cataract?
   A: Both have a 2-year waiting period from first inception, regardless of
      PED status. Listed in Section 6.3 along with Hernia, Hysterectomy,
      Joint Replacement, Varicose Veins, Fistula/Fissure/Piles, and
      Kidney/Gall Bladder Stone removal.
      Source: Section 6.3 — Disease-Specific Waiting Period Table

COMBINED / COMPLEX QUESTIONS (test multi-hop across tables)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Q: For a 55-year-old (AG4) in Zone A with Band D and controlled hypertension,
   what is the approximate annual premium before GST?
   A: Steps:
      1. Base Rate (Band D, Column D, Individual) = 6.10 per Mille
         (Section 5.2)
      2. Zone A Multiplier = 1.20 (Section 5.3) -> 6.10 x 1.20 = 7.32
      3. Age Loading AG4 = +55% (Section 5.4) -> 7.32 x 1.55 = 11.346
      4. PED Loading (controlled hypertension) = +20% (Section 5.9)
         -> 11.346 x 1.20 = 13.6152
      5. Per Mille x Sum Insured = 13.6152 x 25,00,000 / 1000
         = INR 34,038
      Requires: Sections 5.2 + 5.3 + 5.4 + 5.9 + 4.1

Q: What is the maximum Cumulative Bonus available and what happens if a
   claim is made after 4 consecutive claim-free years on Band B?
   A: Maximum Cumulative Bonus is 50% (reached at 5th year and beyond,
      per Section 4.10). After 4 claim-free years, the bonus is 40%. If a
      claim is then made, per Footnote 4.10-a, the bonus reduces by 10
      percentage points at next renewal (40% -> 30%). Note that Cumulative
      Bonus increases the Sum Insured available for claims but does NOT
      increase the Room Rent Sub-Limit (Footnote 4.10-b). If the Sum
      Insured is exhausted, the Restoration Benefit (Clause 4.11) restores
      100% of the original Band Sum Insured once — but not for related
      claims (Footnote 4.11-b) and not available on Band A (Footnote
      4.11-a).
      Requires: Section 4.10 + Footnotes 4.10-a, 4.10-b + Section 4.11 +
      Footnotes 4.11-a, 4.11-b

Q: A 70-year-old in Zone C rated policy is treated in a Zone A hospital
   for a condition that also qualifies as a listed Day Care Procedure.
   They have Band C and disclosed controlled diabetes. Walk through all
   the deductions and co-payments that apply to their claim.
   A: Deductions/co-payments chain:
      1. Day Care Procedure (Section 4.5): 148 listed procedures payable on
         par with inpatient hospitalization — no 24-hour admission needed.
      2. Room Rent Sub-Limit (Section 4.6): Band C = No Sub-Limit (up to
         Single Private Room).
      3. Senior Citizen Co-payment (Section 4.8): Age 66-70 = 15%.
      4. Zone-Based Co-payment (Section 4.9): Zone C-rated treated in
         Zone A = 10%.
      5. These two co-payments are ADDITIVE per Footnote 4.9-a.
      6. Co-payment is applied AFTER Sub-Limit and Proportionate Deduction
         per Footnote 4.8-a, on the net admissible amount.
      7. PED Loading (Section 5.9): Controlled diabetes = +30% (affects
         premium, not claim).
      Requires: Sections 4.5 + 4.6 + 4.8 + 4.9 + Footnotes 4.8-a, 4.9-a
      + Section 5.9

Q: A 5-year-old car with 50% NCB and INR 10,000 voluntary deductible is
   total loss. The car's original price was INR 10,00,000. What is the
   final settlement?
   A: Steps:
      1. IDV depreciation (Clause 4.1): 5-year-old car → 50% depreciation.
         IDV = INR 10,00,000 × 50% = INR 5,00,000.
      2. Total Loss (Clause 8.12): repair cost exceeds 75% of IDV → settlement = IDV.
      3. NCB (Clause 5.6): 50% discount on OD premium, not on IDV.
      4. Voluntary deductible (Clause 5.9): INR 10,000 → 15% discount on OD premium.
      5. Settlement = IDV = INR 5,00,000 (minus any applicable taxes).
      Requires: Clause 4.1 + Clause 8.12 + Clause 5.6 + Clause 5.9

MULTI-DOCUMENT CROSS-REFERENCING QUESTIONS
(Requires information from BOTH MediCare Plus and DriveSecure policies)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Q: Compare the waiting periods: what is the disease-specific waiting period
   for Cataract surgery in the health policy vs. the waiting period for
   voluntary deductible discount in the motor policy?
   A: Health (MediCare Plus): Cataract = 2 years disease-specific waiting
      period (Section 6.3).
      Motor (DriveSecure): Voluntary deductible discount — no waiting
      period, applies immediately (Clause 5.9).
      Cross-referencing: Section 6.3 (health) + Clause 5.9 (motor).

Q: Which policy offers a higher percentage discount for consecutive claim-free
   years: health policy Cumulative Bonus or motor policy NCB?
   A: Health (MediCare Plus): Cumulative Bonus max = 50% (Section 4.10).
      Motor (DriveSecure): NCB max = 50% (Clause 5.6).
      Both cap at 50% but work differently: health bonus increases Sum
      Insured, while motor NCB reduces premium.
      Cross-referencing: Section 4.10 (health) + Clause 5.6 (motor).
